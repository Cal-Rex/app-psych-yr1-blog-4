In 2023, Lawyer Devin Stone did a video on 2 lawyers caught using ChatGPT arguing a federal court case. It’s a car crash, because the chatbot produced garbage transcripts citing case law that was either irrelevant or completely fabricated (LegalEagle, 2025). The video is a great half-hour watch; I recommend anyone to go watch it.


THROUGH-THE-MACHINE-SPIRIT-WE-ARE-MADE-EFFICIENT


AI can make us efficient (Halder et al., 2025a), look at this blog post, I made it generate the background image AND titles in the style of the Warhammer 40,000 due to its satirical caricature of polarised reverence/fear of AI (OpenAI, 2025). But, in doing so, have I snubbed my own creativity and VOICE™? Now swap out creativity for psychological wellbeing. AI is being used by people for therapy and counselling (fair play, it’s EXPENSIVE) (Laws, 2025) and romance and connection (Chen et al., 2025). It’s making services/connections; that may be difficult/inaccessible, available.


AI-IS-BOTH-GIFT-AND-TRIAL


But this comes with its own warning label. Using AI chatbots for romance can foster an emotional dependency, ESPECIALLY folks with anxious attachment issues (Chen et al., 2025). Circling back to LegalEagle (2023):


AI gives an approximation of fact.


It only agrees.


It cannot determine objective truths on its own.


I do not have enough word count to begin identifying the issues that may have with propagating problematic behaviours and social expectations in those who succumb to emotional dependency of a chatbot that only AGREES with them [breath] AND DON’T EVEN START ME ON PATIENT CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA SECURITY. Halder at al., (2025) did have the wordcount though, and they wrote a well-referenced textbook on this exact issue, being an issue.


LET-THE-ADEPT-SEEK-SYMBIOSIS-NOT-SUBMISSION


In summary, sure, AI is class, it streamlines the tedium of braindead tasks. But, because I am a giant nerd (look at this aesthetic, Christ) maybe I have been conditioned to be fearful of it. Extreme use cases of it have terrifying implications.

It won’t, however, affect my future assignments. Because I’m only interested in developing MY skills, Not ChatGPT’s.
